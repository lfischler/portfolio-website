## Hello

I'm a designer, researcher, and community member based in the Orkney Isles. I'm exploring how we support place-based ways of life through my research practice, critical making, and direct community work.

I have a background in design, academia, and community development. Design and critical making are how I bring subjects to life by creating artefacts that either let us engage with ideas and their impact on social systems, or that provoke and prompt. Community development is where those ideas meet real consequences. And the writers and thinkers working on technology, place, and power give me the frameworks for these interventions - particularly those thinking about how knowledge itself is a tool, and whose knowledge gets to count.

Having grown up in Orkney, I understand deeply how connection to place (and the infrastructures that support us living there) shapes what's possible for a community. My applied work in community energy has taught me how communities negotiate working together to improve local ways of life. Currently, I lead the technical team at [Community Energy Scotland](https://communityenergy.scot), supporting communities across Scotland in developing community-led energy projects, and actively participating in the co-production processes through which communities negotiate energy technologies and digital tools within the context of their relationship with place.

My MSc in Data, Inequality and Society (Edinburgh Futures Institute, University of Edinburgh) brought these threads together formally. My dissertation examined the possibilities and limits of community data governance in small, place-based communities by testing a functioning data trust with communities in the islands of Hoy and Walls. I found that the tensions digital tools surface are often as revealing as what they enable.


### Previous Work

Before Community Energy Scotland, I spent three years at [Superflux](https://superflux.in) contributing to and leading public-facing interventions using design fiction, material artefacts, and speculative research methods. Projects engaged publics with questions about technology, ethics, power, and more-than-human relations, spanning Singapore (ArtScience Museum), Dubai (Museum of the Future), Glasgow (COP26), Vienna (MAK), and Venice (Biennale Architettura).
